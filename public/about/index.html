About

    Purpose

    LCToolbox is an open-source linear control toolbox for MATLAB facilitating robust linear feedback controller design. Its main goal is to shield end users (control engineers) from tedious and time-consuming pre- and postprocessing steps that are typically involved in modern controller synthesis (based on H∞ and H2 norms). Both linear time-invariant (LTI) and linear parameter-dependent models are supported.

    The toolbox is, however, not restricted to control design, but strives for being a helpful tool in the entire design procedure of a linear controller. This also includes, among other things, identification of models that are suited for the design of the controller and simulation tools that enable the user verify the performance on nonlinear models.

    Development

    LCToolbox is actively being developed by Maarten Verbandt, Laurens Jacobs, Taranjitsingh Singh and Dora Turk as a part of their PhD research in linear feedback control and identification within the MECO Research Team under supervision of Jan Swevers and Goele Pipeleers.

    Although our resources for support and implementation of custom features are limited, we welcome all feedback, bug reports, feature suggestions, contributions, ... This can go through our Github page or by contacting one of the developers.

    This research is supported by Flanders Make project ROCSIS and is carried out in close collaboration with industrial partners.
